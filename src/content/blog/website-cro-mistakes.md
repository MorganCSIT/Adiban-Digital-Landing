You're ranking on Google. Ads are running. People are clicking through. And then... nothing. No form fills, no calls, no enquiries.

This is one of the most frustrating positions a business can be in — and it's more common than most people realise. The good news is that the causes are almost always diagnosable. Here are the five most common reasons websites get traffic but no leads.

## 1. Your Value Proposition Is Unclear Above the Fold

When someone lands on your homepage, they make a decision within three to five seconds: *is this for me?* If your headline says something like "Welcome to [Company Name]" or "Your trusted partner in [industry]," you've already lost them.

Your above-the-fold section — everything visible before scrolling — needs to answer three questions instantly:
- What do you do?
- Who do you do it for?
- Why should they choose you?

Compare these two headlines:
- **Weak:** "Innovative solutions for modern businesses"
- **Strong:** "BI dashboards and AI tools that help Gauteng manufacturers reduce costs by 15–30%"

The second is specific, targeted, and results-focused. Specificity converts; generality bounces.

## 2. There's No Clear Next Step

If a visitor is interested but can't immediately see what to do next, they leave. This sounds obvious, but a surprising number of business websites bury their call-to-action below the fold, use weak verbs ("Learn more", "Find out more"), or offer too many options and trigger decision paralysis.

Every page should have a single primary CTA. On a services page, that's "Get a Quote" or "Book a Call." On a blog post, that's a contextual CTA related to the topic. On a case study, it's "See How We Can Do This For You."

Strong CTA copy is specific and outcome-focused: "Book a Free 30-Min Strategy Call" converts better than "Contact Us" in almost every test we've run.

## 3. Your Social Proof Is Missing or Generic

"We are a team of passionate professionals committed to excellence" is not social proof. It's filler.

Real social proof is: a testimonial from a named client with a specific result ("They redesigned our site and our enquiry rate went from 8 to 31 per month"), a case study with before/after numbers, or logos of recognisable clients.

If you don't yet have testimonials with numbers, ask your best clients directly. Most are happy to provide them — they just need to be asked.

## 4. Your Page Speed Is Killing You on Mobile

Google's Core Web Vitals data consistently shows that a one-second delay in mobile page load time reduces conversions by up to 20%. Most SME websites we audit load in four to seven seconds on mobile — sometimes worse.

The culprits are almost always the same: uncompressed images, render-blocking JavaScript, unoptimised web fonts, and cheap shared hosting.

Test your site at PageSpeed Insights. If your mobile score is below 60, speed is actively costing you leads.

## 5. You're Sending Traffic to the Wrong Page

This one specifically affects businesses running paid ads. If your ad targets "commercial cleaning services Johannesburg" and it sends people to your homepage — not a dedicated landing page for that specific service in that specific location — you're wasting spend.

Every ad campaign should have a matching landing page with:
- The exact keyword in the headline
- A single CTA (not a full navigation menu)
- Social proof relevant to that service
- No distractions

The mismatch between ad promise and landing page reality is the single biggest conversion killer in paid search for SMEs.

## A Simple Audit Checklist

Before spending another rand on traffic, work through this list:

- [ ] Does your homepage headline state exactly what you do and for whom?
- [ ] Is there a clear, single CTA above the fold?
- [ ] Do you have at least three testimonials with specific results?
- [ ] Does your mobile PageSpeed score exceed 70?
- [ ] Do your paid ads land on dedicated pages, not your homepage?

If you answered "no" to two or more of these, your conversion rate has meaningful room to improve — often without any additional traffic spend.
