Most small and mid-sized businesses have more data than they know what to do with. Sales spreadsheets, accounting exports, CRM reports, Google Analytics dashboards — all open in separate tabs, all manually refreshed, all slightly out of date the moment you print them.

The problem isn't a lack of data. It's a lack of *signal*.

Here are the five KPIs we consistently see undertracked in SMEs — and why getting them into a live dashboard changes the game.

## 1. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

If you don't know what it costs to win a customer, you can't make smart marketing decisions. CAC is calculated simply: total sales and marketing spend divided by new customers acquired in the same period.

The dangerous mistake is calculating CAC company-wide. Break it down by channel — paid search, referrals, organic, events — and you'll almost always find one or two channels that are wildly more efficient than the rest. That's where you double down.

**What to track:** Monthly CAC by channel. Alert when any channel spikes above your average by more than 20%.

## 2. Gross Margin by Product or Service Line

Revenue feels good. Gross margin tells the truth. Many SMEs discover — too late — that their most popular product is also their least profitable one.

Segment margin by SKU, service tier, or customer type. You'll quickly see which work is worth pursuing and which is quietly subsidised by your better-paying clients.

**What to track:** Rolling 3-month gross margin %, segmented. Target a minimum threshold per line.

## 3. Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC)

This is the number of days between paying your suppliers and collecting from your customers. A long CCC means your cash is tied up in operations — a silent killer for growing businesses.

CCC = Days Sales Outstanding + Days Inventory Outstanding − Days Payable Outstanding.

Reducing your CCC by even 10 days can free up significant working capital without a single new sale.

**What to track:** Monthly CCC trend. Watch for creep in DSO — it usually signals a collections problem before it becomes a crisis.

## 4. Lead-to-Close Rate by Stage

Your CRM probably tracks leads. But how many businesses track where exactly those leads drop off?

Mapping conversion rates at each pipeline stage — from first contact to qualified opportunity to proposal to closed — reveals the exact bottleneck in your sales process. For most SMEs, the leak is between proposal and close, often because follow-up cadences aren't systematic.

**What to track:** Stage-by-stage conversion rate, monthly. A drop at any stage is a process problem, not a people problem.

## 5. Net Revenue Retention (NRR)

If you have any recurring revenue — retainers, subscriptions, repeat purchasing — NRR is arguably the most important number in your business. It measures whether existing customers are spending more, the same, or less over time.

NRR above 100% means your base is growing without a single new customer. Below 90% means you're running to stand still.

**What to track:** Monthly NRR. Break it into expansion revenue (upsells), contraction (downgrades), and churn.
